Common Questions

Petfolk urgent care treats non-life-threatening conditions that need prompt attention—like vomiting, minor injuries, ear infections, or sudden limping. Emergency vets handle critical, life-threatening situations like severe trauma, difficulty breathing, or poisoning. For most unexpected health concerns, urgent care provides faster service at up to 40% less cost than an emergency room visit.

Our urgent care team treats a wide range of non-emergency conditions including vomiting and diarrhea, skin irritations and allergies, ear and eye infections, minor wounds and lacerations, urinary issues, limping or sudden mobility changes, and respiratory symptoms like coughing or sneezing.

Urgent care visits at Petfolk can cost up to 40% less than a trip to the emergency vet. Exact pricing varies based on your pet's condition and any diagnostics or treatments needed. Our team will discuss costs with you before proceeding with care.

Yes. Petfolk offers 24/7 medical chat through the Petfolk app, so you can connect with a veterinary professional anytime to discuss your concerns and determine the best next step for your pet.

Absolutely. Petfolk was founded by veterinarians with decades of clinical experience. Our medical teams are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of urgent conditions, and we're equipped with modern diagnostic tools to provide thorough care.

Yes. Petfolk offers same-day veterinary visits with flexible hours. You can book online through the Petfolk app or website, or call us directly to schedule an appointment that fits your day.

Walk-ins are welcome, but we recommend booking an appointment to minimize your wait time and ensure a vet is ready when you arrive. Same-day appointments are typically available, and you can easily schedule through the Petfolk app or by calling your local clinic.

Petfolk provides urgent care during clinic hours for non-life-threatening conditions. If your pet is experiencing a true emergency—such as difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, collapse, or suspected poisoning—please go directly to a 24-hour emergency veterinary hospital.

Bring any relevant medical records (if you have them), a list of current medications your pet takes, and details about when symptoms started. If your pet ingested something, bring the packaging if possible. Having this information helps our team provide faster, more accurate care.

If your pet is eating, drinking, and behaving mostly normally but has a new symptom you want checked out, a regular appointment may be fine. If your pet is in visible discomfort, has stopped eating, is vomiting repeatedly, or has a sudden change in behavior or mobility, same-day urgent care is the better choice. When in doubt, use our 24/7 medical chat to get guidance from a vet.
